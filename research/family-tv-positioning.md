# Family TV — Positioning Document

*Owner: Strategy | Version: 1.0 | Date: 2026-03-30*

---

## Primary Value Proposition

**"Togetherness without proximity."**

Family TV transforms your private family video library into a continuous, shared viewing experience — your own private television channel that plays automatically, keeps everyone watching the same frame at the same moment, and makes distance feel irrelevant.

---

## The Emotional Promise

When your mother in Ohio, your brother in Oregon, and your cousin in London all open Family TV at 7pm, they watch **the same frame of the same video at the same moment**. The video of your daughter's first steps plays for everyone simultaneously — just as if they were all sitting on the same couch.

This is the feeling modern families lose when they scatter across time zones. Family TV brings it back.

---

## Target User — First Customer

**Primary:** New parents with children under 5, where at least one set of grandparents lives in a different state or country.

**Why this user first:**
- They produce enormous amounts of video content (baby milestones, first steps, holidays)
- They desperately want distant grandparents to feel included in their children's lives
- Existing sharing tools (WhatsApp, Google Photos) make video sharing chaotic, ephemeral, and impossible to watch together
- They have high motivation to pay for a solution that solves this specific pain
- They are active content creators who will populate the channel immediately

**Secondary first customers:**
- Small families (2-4 adults) with distant elderly parents who want to share memories
- Families who have lost a loved one and want a shared space to preserve and experience memories together

**Not the first customer:**
- Large extended families (15+ members) — too complex to onboard initially
- Families primarily sharing photos — video-first is core differentiator, still developing photo use case
- Parents of teenagers — content ownership and sharing dynamics are more complicated

## What Family TV Does That TimeTree/Cozi Cannot

| Competitor | Core Model | What They Can't Do |
|------------|------------|---------------------|
| **TimeTree** | Shared calendar for families | Video is a background feature. No sync, no channel metaphor, no broadcast semantics. Their UX is coordination; Family TV is shared experience. |
| **Cozi** | Family organizer (calendar, lists, reminders) | Not video-focused at all. No sync infrastructure. Adding video would feel bolted-on and conflict with their organizer mental model. |
| **Google Photos** | Personal video storage + sharing | Videos are files in a folder. No shared viewing, no sync, no sense that "something is always on." |
| **WhatsApp/Signal** | Chat + file sending | Videos are attachments. Downloads expire. No shared context, no schedule, no ambient "channel." |

**Family TV's differentiation in plain language:**
- TimeTree and Cozi help you **coordinate** your family. Family TV helps you **experience** your family.
- Family TV is the only product that treats your family videos as **programming**, not files.
- Family TV is the only product where the TV is **always on**, always waiting, always playing together — even when no one's watching.

## Pricing Model

### Free Tier — "Family Channel Starter"

*For: One family trying Family TV for the first time*

- **1 family channel** (no sub-channels)
- **Up to 5 family members** (including the founding member)
- **10 GB video storage** (~20-30 typical family videos)
- **720p video quality** maximum
- **Basic synchronized playback** (play/pause/seek within 500ms sync)
- **Solo mode** — watch independently but stay in the channel
- **Presence indicators** — see who's watching in real-time
- **Standard queue** — up to 5 videos in Up Next

**Limits:** Not embarrassing. A new family with a newborn recording milestone videos will fit comfortably in 10GB for 6+ months. This is the "try it and see" tier that converts.

---

### Premium Tier — "Family Channel Plus" — $9.99/month

*For: Families who have made Family TV their primary way of sharing memories*

- **Everything in Free, plus:**
- **Up to 5 sub-channels** (e.g., "Grandma's Favorites," "Kids Channel," "Henderson Cousins")
- **Up to 15 family members**
- **100 GB video storage** (~200-300 family videos)
- **4K / HDR video quality**
- **Extended video length** — up to 30-minute videos (vs. 10-minute free limit)
- **Family calendar integration** — events from Cozi/TimeTree appear as "programming" on the TV Guide
- **Catch-up notifications** — get notified when you missed a live session with a "rewatch" prompt
- **Channel customization** — custom channel callsign, avatar, theme color
- **Priority sync** — <300ms sync latency (vs. standard <500ms)
- **Multi-device casting** — cast from phone to TV while controlling from phone

**Rationale for $9.99:** Family plans on Spotify ($15.99), Apple One ($25+/month), and streaming services show families are willing to spend $10-20/month on shared experiences. The value comparison is "one streaming subscription = a private family channel that's actually watching together."

## Emotional Tagline Options

**Option 1 — Experience-focused:**
"The living room you can share across any distance."

*Why it works:* Directly answers the core emotional need — proximity replaced by shared experience. Pairs the familiar warmth of "living room" with the spatial impossibility Family TV overcomes.

---

**Option 2 — Channel-focused:**
"Your family's private channel, always on."

*Why it works:* Names the product category ("channel") and the unique value ("always on" — meaning it's waiting even when you're not). Implies the ambient, passive nature of the experience.

---

**Option 3 — Memory-focused:**
"Watch your memories together, even when you're apart."

*Why it works:* Frames family videos as living memories rather than archives. "Together" is the emotional core; "apart" acknowledges the distance problem Family TV solves.

---

## Positioning Statement (For Marketing)

> Family TV is a private family video channel — not a storage app, not a chat thread, not a social feed. It turns your family's video library into a continuously-playing shared experience that feels like watching television together, even when you're hundreds of miles apart. No algorithms. No strangers. Just your family, watching your moments, together.
